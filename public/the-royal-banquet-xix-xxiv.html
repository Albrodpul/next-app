Dear friends,
On Thursday, 13 November, as Madrid prepared to host its first-ever NFL regular-season game, a different kind of rhythm took over the Sol Ballroom at the Four Seasons.
The Royal Banquet 19.24 was not a dinner. It was a cocktail party in motion — the opening of the Beyond the Game: Madrid Without Limits experience, and the start of something new: a way of celebrating cities not just with spectacle, but with presence, emotion, and meaning.
To those who joined us: thank you. Your presence — your way of moving through the room, connecting, listening, and shaping the energy — made that night what it was. To those who could not attend, you were very much in our minds. This was only the first of many.
As the evening unfolded, dancers emerged from the crowd, moving from the elegance of classical forms to the raw expression of flamenco, their elaborate costumes evolving with the story they told. Each passage mirrored a journey through time and culture, guided by an award-winning DJ and live musicians who built a soundscape that transcended eras. There was no stage. The room itself was the performance.
Michelin-starred chef Diego Guerrero and his team sent out a sequence of exquisite small plates, designed not to interrupt the moment but to carry it forward. Each bite marked a new beat. The lighting shifted almost imperceptibly. Music threaded between guests. And in between it all, a new kind of celebration took shape — one grounded in people, not protocol; in conversation, not hierarchy.
But the evening was just the beginning.
Over several days, guests were welcomed with private airport protocols and discreet transfers, attended by 24-hour liaisons who ensured every detail unfolded without interruption. The experience moved through intimate encounters: watchmaking with IWC, private shopping within Galería Canalejas and Loewe, and exclusive appointments at Cartier, Hermès, and Chopard. Tastings featuring Dalmore whisky and Sampaka chocolate added depth, while Michelin-starred restaurant dinners created true gastronomic odysseys across the city. Woven throughout was a quiet but vital thread: a shared commitment to causes like Cris for Cancer, whose presence reminded us that meaning elevates even the most exquisite experiences.
All of it culminated with a defining moment in sports history: the first-ever American football game played in Spain, experienced not in isolation, but as the final chapter in a layered cultural narrative.
We’re proud that Robb Report en Español captured the atmosphere in their feature, “Paradise a La Carte firma en Madrid el encuentro más exclusivo previo al histórico debut de la NFL en España.”
